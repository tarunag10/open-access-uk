# Governance

Open Access UK uses lightweight maintainer governance while the project is
early-stage.

## Roles

- Maintainers decide release readiness, review high-risk changes, and protect
  project principles.
- Contributors can propose fixes, issues, docs, source updates, tests, and UI
  improvements.
- Reviewers may be asked to check accessibility, source provenance, legal-risk
  wording, or public-service workflows.

## Decision Rules

- Keep the suite static and browser-only by default.
- Privacy, accessibility, and source-risk concerns can block a release.
- High-risk content needs source notes and maintainer review.
- Backends, analytics, storage, accounts, or external data collection require a
  separate public review before implementation.

## Urgent Fixes

Maintainers may fast-track fixes for privacy leaks, XSS, misleading high-risk
guidance, broken public links, or accessibility barriers that seriously affect
use of the tools.

## Content-Risk Approval

Changes to legal templates, rights wording, complaint deadlines, ombudsman
routes, or other action-guiding public-service content should include:

- Official or specialist source links.
- Last-checked date.
- Reviewer.
- Clear safety note where needed.
